About

Practice grounded in light, proportion, and context

Yasaman Jalalian is an architect interested in how buildings choreograph daily life—how thresholds, daylight, and material calm shape the feeling of a place. Her work spans residential studies, small public interventions, and analytical mapping projects that read cities as layered stories rather than abstract grids.

Each proposal begins with careful measurement of context: climate, circulation, heritage, and the informal rituals already present on site. Design moves stay quiet on purpose—clear plans, generous sections, and detailing that ages gracefully.
